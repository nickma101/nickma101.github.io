I am a PhD candidate at the Vrije Universiteit Amsterdam. I research news selection and exposure diversity in news recommender systems (NRSs). The goal of my research is to a) model individual news selection behaviour in NRSs and b) explore presentation strategies that could be used by the designers of such systems to facilitate exposure diversity while also taking into account potential backfire effects and ethical implications. I have the privilige of working in an [interdisciplinary team of excellent researchers](http://ccs.amsterdam/projects/rethinking-news-algorithms-nudging-users-towards-diverse-news-exposure/), which enables me to also tap into the ethical and NLP challenges that come with news recommender design.

Apart from that, my research interests include political communication, journalism, media psychology, and computational social science. You can find all of my work on this website or on my [google scholar profile](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=OuJCoVcAAAAJ&hl=nl&inst=4393003693960974403&oi=ao).

When I am not behind my laptop, I like to play music (electric bass and guitar), read (science) fiction, or catch up on the latest indie movies.
